Academic Experiences and Focus

I earned my Bachelors in Industrial Engineering and my Masters and PhD in Operations Research at NC State in Raleigh, NC. My training as an industrial engineer gave me the basic tools for problems associated with production and service systems: factory design and planning, inventory management, service system planning, supply chain optimization, forecasting, and the like.

My graduate work took me deeper into the fundamental methods that underpin most of these problems. In my disseration I study the application of linear and mixed integer programming principles to matching theory, a subfield of Microeconomics. The primary research questions are concerned with fairly allocating scarce, indivisible goods to diverse populations. Common applications include school choice problems where students have preferences over which school they are assigned to and the schools have affirmative action style policies by which they must abide.

Professional Experiences

Through my academic career I worked a variety of internships and co-ops at companies including Hanesbrands and Tesla. While in grad school, I worked with NCGrowth doing pro-bono consulting for small, local manufacturers in NC. I funded part of my PhD with the SMART Scholarship via the DoD, which led me to my current position with the Army Futures Command as an Operations Analyst.

In my time at Hanesbrands, I worked with their logistics group to develop a tool that forecasts the shipping requirements at all ports given the previously agreed upon production schedule. Hanesbrands has production facilities across the world, many of which are in southeast Asia. My work offered to reduce shipping costs by arming Hanesbrands with better estimates of how many shipping containers would be required at each port, thus improving their negotiating position with carriers.

My work at Tesla gave me a broad range of manufacturing experience. In the Gigafactory in Sparks, NV, I started working with one of the more mature assembly lines where I developed live dashboards in Tableau to calculate and communicate machine availability. The second half of my co-op was spent with an assembly line that was under ongoing construction. In the shuffle, I secured the project of designing and testing prototypes of an assembly line that would serve as a temporary solution while the main line was down. The final iteration of my design was green-lit by higher management and implemented to meet production goals.

Working with NCGrowth allowed me to apply the lessons and principles learned at Tesla to traditional manufacturing environments. I used time and motion studies to design assembly lines for a small furniture manufacturer in High Point, NC. These and other methods reduced the overall variability suffered by the system, which in turn reduced cycle times and improved production capacity.

Working with the Army Future Command has been an excellent experience. My team's analysis contributes to planning efforts for the US Army of 2035 and beyond by suggesting requirements, assessing risk, and highlighting critical areas for operational level improvements. Many of our studies are carried out via large scale discrete event simulations and the attendant data analytics. The problems we face are often wide-ranging and ill-defined, requiring novel approaches and highly functional working relationships with external stakeholders and decision makers.
